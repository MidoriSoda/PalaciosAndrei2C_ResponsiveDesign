The Town's History

In 1579, Spanish Franciscan missionaries leaded by Father Estevan Ortis arrived in the island. He imbedded a sacred cross as a symbol of the beginning of Christianity in Marinduque. Not long after, the Jesuits arrived in 1581. Spanish priests also noticed that the island is now well populated than before and it is estimated that there are 2, 800. The Franciscans built a church made of red brick in Santa Cruz and named it as San Juan de Bondoc as the patron of the town is San Juan. Beside is an old two-story convent and later on, the Franciscan infirmary transferred here in the 1600s and in 1609, it was founded as a pueblo. Transportation

The main public transportation in the town is by means of jeepneys and tricycle. These public transportation has designated destinations per organization (cooperatives and association) for short and long travel commutes.

A Reminder for Motorcyle Riders

There is a public ordinance in the town for wearing helmet in the vicinity of Poblacion. Due to a shooting incident, an ordinance was issued that wearing helmets are not allowed in the vicinity of Poblacion.

Land and Population

The municipality has a land area of 270.77 square kilometers or 104.54 square miles which constitutes 28.42% of Marinduque's total area. Its population as determined by the 2020 Census was 54,692. This represented 22.86% of the total population of Marinduque province, or 1.69% of the overall population of the MIMAROPA Region.
